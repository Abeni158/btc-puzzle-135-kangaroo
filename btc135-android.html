How Kangaroo Works
1. Two herds of kangaroos — Wild ones jump randomly in the key range. Tame ones start from a known point and jump deterministically.

2. Distinguished Points (DP) — When a kangaroo lands on a position where the lowest 16 bits are 0, it records its position and distance traveled.

3. Birthday collision — Eventually a wild and tame kangaroo land on the same DP. This reveals the private key via math on their distances.

4. Square root speedup — Instead of 2¹³⁵ operations, only ~2⁶⁷·⁵ are needed. Still massive — requires GPU clusters.
